## Problem Statement 
---
Nigeria continues to face persistent challenges in managing HIV/AIDS, particularly in the areas of early detection, treatment accessibility, and patient outcomes. Between 2023 and 2025, disparities have emerged across regions and healthcare facilities, with inconsistent testing rates, uneven treatment coverage, and varying levels of patient adherence. At the same time, the financial burden of care especially out-of-pocket payments remains a critical barrier, limiting access to sustained treatment for many individuals.

A major issue is the lack of an integrated data-driven approach that connects patient demographics, treatment patterns, financial contributions, and facility performance. Without this unified perspective, it is difficult for healthcare stakeholders to accurately identify high-risk populations, evaluate the effectiveness of treatment options such as ART, PrEP, and Lenacapavir, or measure how financial constraints influence treatment outcomes.

This project addresses these gaps by analyzing interconnected datasets to uncover trends, inefficiencies, and disparities in HIV care. The goal is to provide actionable insights that can improve healthcare delivery, reduce financial strain on patients, and enhance overall treatment effectiveness across Nigeria.

## Project Objectives 
---
 1. Analyze HIV Trends and Patient Distribution:
This project aims to examine HIV trends across Nigeria between 2023 and 2025 by analyzing patient demographics, infection rates, and geographic distribution. By leveraging the Patients table, the objective is to identify high-risk populations based on age, gender, and location. Understanding these patterns will help reveal regions with increasing prevalence and highlight gaps in early detection. The analysis will also track yearly changes in HIV cases to determine whether interventions are improving outcomes or if new strategies are required. This insight is crucial for public health planning and targeted awareness programs.

 2. Evaluate Treatment Effectiveness and Patient Outcomes:
The project seeks to assess how different treatment types influence patient outcomes and adherence levels. Using the KPI table, it will analyze the relationship between adherence rates and outcomes such as improved, stable, or critical conditions. The goal is to determine which treatments ART, PrEP, or Lenacapavir deliver the most effective results. This will help in understanding whether higher adherence leads to better health outcomes and where improvements in treatment delivery are needed.

 3. Assess Financial Burden and Payment Patterns:
A key objective is to evaluate the financial impact of HIV treatment on patients by analyzing payment data, including total costs, insurance contributions, and out-of-pocket expenses. The project will explore how payment methods and insurance coverage influence access to treatment and adherence levels. By identifying cost trends and financial barriers, this analysis will provide insight into affordability challenges and support recommendations for reducing patient burden and improving healthcare accessibility.

 4. Measure Healthcare Facility Performance:
This project aims to assess the performance of healthcare facilities by analyzing testing volumes, positivity rates, and treatment coverage across different states and LGAs. Using the Facilities table, it will identify high-performing and underperforming facilities. The goal is to uncover inefficiencies in service delivery and highlight areas where healthcare infrastructure needs improvement. This will support better resource allocation and policy decisions.

 5. Generate Data-Driven Insights for Decision Making:
The final objective is to integrate all datasets to generate actionable insights that support healthcare decision making. By combining patient, financial, and facility data, the project will uncover relationships between cost, treatment, and outcomes. The goal is to provide a comprehensive view that enables stakeholders to improve treatment strategies, optimize resource distribution, and enhance overall HIV care delivery in Nigeria.

## Exploratory Data Analysis
---
The exploratory data analysis for this project focuses on understanding the structure, quality, and key patterns within the HIV dataset across Nigeria from 2023 to 2025. The process begins with data cleaning, addressing inconsistencies such as missing values, duplicate records, and irregular text formats across the Patients, KPI (Payments), and Facilities tables. Standardization of variables like gender, treatment type, and payment methods ensures consistency for accurate analysis.

Descriptive statistics are then applied to summarize key variables, including patient age distribution, viral load levels, treatment costs, and facility testing volumes. Trends are explored across years to identify changes in HIV cases, financial contributions, and treatment outcomes.

Overall, the EDA provides a foundational understanding of the dataset, identifies key patterns and anomalies, and prepares the data for deeper analysis and visualization, ensuring that subsequent insights are both accurate and meaningful.
